BTO & New Flats
BTO eligibility — full criteria▼
SC + age 21+ (family) or 35+ (single, 2-room Flexi). Income ceiling $14K (most) or $7K (2-room Flexi). Must not own any property.Additional rules:
• Must not have bought 2 HDB flats before
• Must form valid family nucleus
• Multi-generation families: $21K ceiling
• Extended family: $21K ceiling
• Plus/Prime flats may have stricter rules per launch

What are Standard, Plus, Prime flats?▼
New BTO classification from Oct 2024. Standard = regular BTO. Plus = better locations, tighter rules. Prime = best locations, tightest rules.Standard: 5-yr MOP, no clawback
Plus: 10-yr MOP, 6-8% subsidy clawback on resale, resale buyer must meet $14K ceiling
Prime: 10-yr MOP, 9% clawback, resale buyer must meet $14K ceiling, 30-month wait-out for private property owners

Both Plus & Prime: whole-flat rental banned even after MOP.
✓ HDB Standard, Plus, and Prime Housing Framework page
✓ Verified via HDB HIGHNewBuyer Meeting a Buyer
Subsidy clawback — how does it actually work?▼
When Plus/Prime owner sells after MOP, they pay HDB a % of resale price (or valuation, whichever is higher).Rates set per launch: Oct 2024 launch was 6–8% for Plus, 9% for Prime.
Example: Prime flat sold at $1.2M with 9% clawback = $108K paid back to HDB.
Important: clawback only applies to the ORIGINAL BTO owner. Resale buyer of a Plus/Prime flat doesn't pay clawback when they later sell.

What is MOP?▼
Minimum Occupation Period. Standard HDB: 5 years. Plus/Prime: 10 years. Starts from key collection date.During MOP, owner cannot: sell, rent whole flat (rooms OK), buy/own private property.

Selling Price − Outstanding Loan − CPF P+I (all owners) − Agent commission − Legal fees − SSD (if any) = Net cashRun this for every seller at first meeting. Many sellers are shocked when they realise 'paper gain' doesn't equal 'cash in pocket' after CPF refund.

Negative sale — how is CPF refund split?▼
Pro-rated based on each owner's P+I share of total.Formula: Your refund = (Your P+I ÷ Total P+I) × Net proceeds

Example: Net $160K. Husband P+I $150K, Wife P+I $50K. Total $200K.
Husband: 150/200 × $160K = $120K
Wife: 50/200 × $160K = $40K
Neither gets cash. Buyer's deposit must also go to sellers' CPF.

The 'age 95' rule — full CPF pro-ration formula▼
Youngest buyer's age + remaining lease ≥ 95 → FULL CPF. If < 95 → pro-rated.Pro-ration formula: (Remaining Lease − 20) ÷ (95 − Youngest Age − 20) × VL

Examples:
Age 25 + 88yr = 113 → Full
Age 25 + 65yr = 90 → 45/50 = 90%
Age 45 + 50yr = 95 → Full (exactly 95 counts)
Age 50 + 40yr = 90 → 20/25 = 80%

From 10 May 2019.
